World Geography

Earth is divided into seven continents: Africa, Antarctica, Asia, Australia (Oceania), Europe, North America, and South America. Asia is the largest continent by both area and population. Antarctica is the coldest, driest, and windiest continent and has no permanent human inhabitants.

The world has five oceans: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic. The Pacific Ocean is the largest, covering more area than all land on Earth combined. The Arctic Ocean is the smallest and shallowest.

Russia is the largest country by land area, spanning 17.1 million square kilometres and crossing 11 time zones. The Vatican City is the smallest country in the world, located within Rome, Italy.

The equator is an imaginary line that divides Earth into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. Countries on the equator include Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Kenya, and Indonesia. The equatorial region generally has a tropical climate with high temperatures and rainfall year-round.

Mount Everest, located in the Himalayas on the border of Nepal and Tibet, is the world's highest peak at 8,849 metres above sea level. The Mariana Trench in the Pacific Ocean is the deepest point on Earth at approximately 11,034 metres below sea level.

The Amazon River in South America is the largest river by discharge volume. The Nile River in Africa has traditionally been considered the longest river, though some measurements give that distinction to the Amazon. The Amazon Basin contains the world's largest tropical rainforest.

The Sahara Desert in northern Africa is the world's largest hot desert, covering about 9 million square kilometres. The Antarctic Ice Sheet is the largest cold desert. Deserts receive less than 250 mm of precipitation per year.

The International Date Line runs roughly along the 180° meridian in the Pacific Ocean. Crossing it travelling eastward subtracts one calendar day; travelling westward adds one day.
