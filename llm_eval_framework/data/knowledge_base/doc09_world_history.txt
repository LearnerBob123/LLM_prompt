World History: Key Events and Eras

Human civilisation has developed over approximately 12,000 years since the Agricultural Revolution, when early humans transitioned from nomadic hunter-gatherers to settled farming communities around 10,000 BCE.

Among the earliest civilisations were those in Mesopotamia (present-day Iraq), Egypt, the Indus Valley (present-day Pakistan and India), and China. Mesopotamia, between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, is often called the "cradle of civilisation" and produced early writing (cuneiform), law codes (including Hammurabi's Code, c. 1754 BCE), and city-states.

Ancient Greece (c. 800–146 BCE) laid intellectual foundations for Western philosophy, democracy, science, and the arts. Athens introduced one of the earliest forms of democratic governance. The conquests of Alexander the Great spread Greek culture across the Middle East, Persia, and into India.

The Roman Empire at its peak controlled much of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The Western Roman Empire fell in 476 CE, traditionally marking the start of the Middle Ages. The Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire continued until 1453 CE.

The Black Death (bubonic plague) of the 14th century killed an estimated 30–60% of Europe's population. The Renaissance (14th–17th centuries) followed, bringing renewed interest in arts, science, and humanism.

European colonisation from the 15th century onward had profound global impacts, including the Atlantic slave trade, which forcibly transported an estimated 12.5 million Africans to the Americas between the 16th and 19th centuries.

The Industrial Revolution, beginning in Britain around 1760, transformed manufacturing through mechanisation, steam power, and factory production, reshaping economies and societies worldwide.

World War I (1914–1918) and World War II (1939–1945) were the deadliest conflicts in human history. WWII resulted in approximately 70–85 million deaths. The Holocaust, perpetrated by Nazi Germany, killed approximately 6 million Jewish people and millions of others.

The Cold War (1947–1991) was a geopolitical rivalry between the United States and the Soviet Union. The dissolution of the USSR in 1991 marked the end of the Cold War era.
